Privacy Policy

Last updated: 14 July 2026

The Listening Room Counselling Service ("we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what personal information we collect through this website, why we collect it, how we use it, and your rights regarding it.

Who we are

The Listening Room Counselling Service is operated by Michelle Welch. If you have any questions about this policy or how your data is handled, you can contact us using the details at the bottom of this page.

What information we collect

When you use the contact form on this website, we collect:

  • Your name
  • Your email address
  • Any message or information you choose to include, which may include sensitive personal details relating to your health or wellbeing

We do not use cookies or tracking technologies to monitor your behaviour on this site beyond basic, privacy-respecting search visibility tools (see our Cookie Policy for details).

Why we collect it and our legal basis

We collect this information so that we can respond to your enquiry about counselling services. Because enquiries may include details about your mental health or personal circumstances, this may count as "special category data" under UK GDPR. We only process this information:

  • With your consent, given by voluntarily submitting the contact form, and
  • Where necessary to take steps at your request prior to entering into a professional counselling arrangement.

How your information is stored

Enquiries submitted through this website are processed and stored securely by our website hosting provider, Netlify, using their form-handling service. We review and respond to enquiries directly and do not sell, rent, or share your information with third parties for marketing purposes.

How long we keep your information

We retain enquiry submissions only for as long as necessary to respond to you and, where a counselling relationship begins, in line with professional and ethical record-keeping guidance (e.g. BACP guidance). Enquiries that do not lead to further contact are deleted periodically, and in any case no longer than 12 months after submission.

Your rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Ask what personal data we hold about you
  • Ask us to correct or update inaccurate data
  • Ask us to delete your data
  • Withdraw consent at any time
  • Complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) if you believe your data has been mishandled

To exercise any of these rights, contact us using the details below.
