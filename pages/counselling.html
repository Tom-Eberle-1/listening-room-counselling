What we offer

Counselling services

Whether you are looking for traditional talking therapy or something more grounded in nature, we offer a range of approaches to suit different people and different needs.

Our services

How we can help

For adults

Individual Counselling

One-to-one sessions with Michelle, offering a private and confidential space to talk through whatever is on your mind. There is no agenda and no judgement — just an honest, supported conversation at your pace.

Sessions typically last 50 minutes and take place at the practice near Hawkstone Park. Regular weekly sessions are recommended initially, though this is always flexible.

Book a Session
Outdoors with horses

Equine Assisted Therapy

Equine assisted therapy takes place outdoors, working alongside our horses in a safe and supported environment. You do not need any experience with horses — sessions are not about riding, but about connection, presence and self-awareness.

Horses are highly sensitive and respond to emotion in the moment, offering a kind of honest, non-verbal feedback that can be genuinely powerful for people who find traditional talking therapy difficult.

Book a Session
For children and young people

Support for Young People

Michelle has extensive experience working with children and young people, including many years in school environments. Sessions are tailored to the young person's age and needs, using nature, animals and creative approaches where helpful.

If you are a parent or carer looking for support for a child or young person, please get in touch for an initial conversation about what might work best.

Who counselling is for

You do not need a crisis to come to counselling

Counselling is for anyone who wants a safe, private space to talk. People come to The Listening Room for many different reasons, including:

Anxiety and worry

Depression and low mood

Bereavement and loss

Relationship difficulties

Low confidence or self-esteem

Stress and burnout

Life transitions and change

Coping with illness

Eating disorders

Divorce and separation

Exam stress

Anger and sleep difficulties

The process

What to expect

01

Get in touch

Send a message or call to have an initial conversation. There is no commitment at this stage — just a chance to ask questions and see if it feels right.

02

First session

The first session is a chance to meet, talk through what has brought you to counselling, and decide together how you would like to proceed.

03

Ongoing sessions

Sessions are typically weekly and last 50 minutes. The number of sessions varies — some people benefit from a short course, others prefer longer term support.
