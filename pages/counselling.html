Outdoors with horses

Equine Assisted Counselling & Learning

Equine assisted counselling and learning offers a unique, experiential way to support emotional wellbeing and personal development. Through ground-based activities with horses, you gain insight into your thoughts, feelings, and behaviours — supported by the horse's natural sensitivity to human emotion.

Sessions take place outdoors, working alongside the horses — not riding them. There is no experience required and nothing to prepare. Horses are honest, present, and non-judgmental. They respond to how you are feeling in the moment, not how you think you should be feeling.

We offer one-to-one sessions for children, young people and adults, as well as group sessions of up to six. Programmes can be tailored to individual needs, and Michelle has extensive experience working with primary and secondary schools, SEN departments, residential children's homes, and wider organisations.

Room-based & online

Individual Counselling

I offer counselling for adults, children, and young people — providing a safe, confidential space to explore whatever is causing difficulty or distress. People come for many reasons: depression, anxiety, bereavement, loss, separation, abuse, trauma, PTSD, low self-esteem, and relationship issues.

My work begins with understanding your experience in the present. Using a person-centred approach, I listen carefully to what is happening for you now, helping you explore your thoughts, feelings, and challenges at your own pace. From there, we may look at how past experiences influence your current situation.

As insight grows, we begin to look towards the future. Solution-focused techniques can help shift your perspective from feeling stuck to seeing possibilities — identifying goals, exploring new ways of thinking, and taking steps towards meaningful change.

For children & young people

Support for Young People

Working with children and young people requires a different approach. They do not always have the language to express what they are feeling, so Michelle uses creative and therapeutic methods — art, craft, play, sand tray work, and the animals themselves. These approaches allow children to communicate their inner world in a way that feels natural and safe.

Michelle has many years of experience supporting children and teenagers through anxiety, bereavement, behavioural challenges, SEN needs, autism, ADHD, and PTSD. She is also an external provider for a local Academy Trust and is DSL Safeguarding trained, which strengthens her work across educational and multi-agency settings.

If you are a parent or carer looking for support for a child or young person, please get in touch for an initial conversation about what might work best.
