About the practice

Counselling that meets you
where you are

The Listening Room has been offering calm, relational support to children, young people, adults, and practitioners since 2012 — outdoors, indoors, and online.

Founder & Lead Counsellor

Michelle Welch

Hello, I'm Michelle. I'm a MBACP Accredited counsellor and clinical supervisor, and I founded The Listening Room in 2012 with a belief that some of the most meaningful conversations happen not in a room — but outdoors, in the company of animals.

I work with children, young people, adults, and fellow practitioners — in person, online, and through equine-assisted sessions with my lifelong herd. My practice is shaped by many years of experience across schools, residential children's homes, and wider support networks.

Whatever you bring to a session, my aim is always the same: to create a grounded, reflective space where you feel safe enough to breathe, explore, and grow.

How I work

My approach

My practice is integrative — drawing on person-centred therapy, CBT, systemic thinking, solution-focused work, and psychodynamic understanding. I use whatever is most helpful for you, at the pace that feels right.

Person-centred

There is no fixed script. Every session is shaped entirely around you — what you bring, what you need, and what feels right at the time.

Integrative

Drawing on CBT, systemic thinking, solution-focused and psychodynamic approaches, I adapt to you — not the other way around.

Nature-based

The outdoors has a unique power to support emotional wellbeing. Equine-assisted sessions take full advantage of working in a calm, natural setting.

Without judgement

Whatever you bring to a session, you will be met with honesty, warmth, and complete confidentiality. Always at your pace.

What does MBACP Accredited mean? BACP Accreditation is the highest professional recognition available to counsellors in the UK. It requires a minimum of 450 supervised hours of practice, advanced training, and ongoing professional development — and must be renewed regularly. When you work with Michelle, you are working with one of the most rigorously qualified counsellors in the country.

For fellow practitioners

Clinical Supervision

I have been a clinical supervisor for many years and value the variety, depth, and professional connection it brings. I particularly enjoy supporting trainee counsellors as they grow in confidence and move toward qualification.

My approach is integrative — grounded in the person-centred model and informed by CBT, systemic thinking, and the Seven Eyed Model of Supervision. I work within the BACP Ethical Framework and follow the professional standards of HETI.

I also offer equine-assisted counselling supervision for practitioners who incorporate horses into their therapeutic work. Supervision is available monthly or fortnightly, online and in person.

Ask about supervision

Supervision can help you

  • Explore what feels stuck in client work

  • Reflect on new or challenging clinical issues

  • Think through CPD, accreditation, or qualification pathways

  • Consider the practical and emotional aspects of private practice

  • Process personal material activated through client work

  • Receive specialist equine-assisted counselling supervision
