What is equine assisted counselling?

You don't need to know anything about horses

Equine assisted counselling combines the skill of a trained therapist with the natural sensitivity of horses. Sessions take place outdoors, working alongside the horses — not riding them. There is no experience required and nothing to prepare.

Horses are honest, present and non-judgmental. They respond to how you are feeling in the moment, not how you think you should be feeling. For many people, this creates a kind of connection that is difficult to find in a traditional therapy room — and makes it easier to open up.

Sessions are calm, unhurried and led entirely by you. Michelle is there throughout, guiding and supporting at every step.

Lead Counsellor & Founder

Michelle Welch

Michelle founded The Listening Room with a belief that some of the most powerful therapeutic work happens not in a room, but outdoors — in the company of animals.

She is a Registered MBACP (Accredited) counsellor with a Foundation Degree in Counselling & Psychotherapy and a Level 6 qualification in Supervision. She has worked with adults, children and young people across private practice and school settings throughout Shropshire.

Michelle works with people navigating anxiety, depression, grief, relationship difficulties, life transitions and more — always at your pace, always without judgement.
